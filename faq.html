Frequently Asked Questions



How do I place my order?

To start placing your order follow the steps listed on our homepage. After you have an idea of what you want out of your cake creation, give us a call. We usually prefer to meet in person to finalize designs and ingredients.


How much time in advance should I order?

We request that you order your cake 2 weeks in advance. For wedding cakes, we request at least one month.


Is there a limit to how customized my cake can be?

We are always open to creative ideas. We can accomodate ingredient replacements for refined sugar, dairy, wheat, etc. Organic ingredients are also available. Your cake creation can be created in almost any shape you desire (letters, numbers, hearts, stars, etc.). Take a look at our Cake Gallery to view example cake creations. We're always happy to discuss your dream cake no matter how ambitious.


Do I need to put down a deposit?

Yes. We require a 50% non-refundable deposit in order to confirm your order. As soon as the deposit is received, your cake creation order will officially be confirmed!


Can I make changes to my order once it has been confirmed?

You can make changes to your order as long as the request is made at least 7 days prior to the date of pick up or delivery. If your cake is more intricate (such as most wedding cakes), we request that you let us know of any changes at least two weeks in advance.


How does delivery work?

We can deliver your cake creation anywhere in the New York Metropolitan Area including parts of New Jersey and Long Island. However, there is a cost associated with delivery depending on the distance. We also offer pickup at our location. If you choose to pickup, please make sure that you have a safe way of transporting the cake (i.e. flat surface without the cake sliding around).


Should I keep my cake creation refrigerated?

Depending on the type of cake you have selected, you may have to refrigerate your cake. If you have chosen any variety of buttercream frosting, you should keep the cake refrigerated in order to keep the frosting intact. However, if you have chosen fondant on your cake, avoid refrigerating your cake creation. The moisture from the fridge can damage the fondant. Nonetheless, make sure a fondant coated cake is stored in a dry place less than 70 degrees.
